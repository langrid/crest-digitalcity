Old 4-track stuff

These are some short 4-track recordings I made in my apartment while I was living in Brooklyn. I don't like them that much because the playing is kind of sloppy, but I found them lying around so I decided to put them on a web page as I don't have anything new to show.
So here they are:
fragment1 This is the second thing I did with my 4-track. It's 2 tracks of my telecaster, one track of jazz bass, and one drum track provided by Dr. Rythem. Maybe this could be called southern rock?
fragment2 This is the first thing I recorded after buying my 4-track. Like the recording above it 2 tracks tele, 1 track jazz bass, and Dr. Rythem is on the drum track again. Pretty lame punk-ish stuff going on.
fragment3 This might be the recording I'm least ashamed of. A little 7/4 math rock-ish thing. What I like most is the distorted jazz bass sound. This is 3 tracks of my Jag, jazz bass, and Dr. Rythem.
fragment4 Don't really remember when I did this, but I know I woke up one morning and had this idea. I recorded it quickly before I went to work. When i got home I listened to it again and didn't like it. This time it's one track of Jaguar, one jazz bass, and a lazy Dr. Rythem track.
fragment5 My friend's band had this song that I thought was interesting but I thought it would be cooler if it was faster then they did it, in an odd meter, and with some electronic instruments. I think I was wrong. Here is a version of the Moonshine Messiahs, my friend's band, doing their song.
