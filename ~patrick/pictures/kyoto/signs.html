Kentucky Fried Chicken places in Japan all sport a stylish Colonel Sanders out front. I think he's one of the only other Americans in my neighborhood. I kind of want to get a fake chicken and put it in the Colonel's hands. Or maybe a real one...
This Colonel Sanders is a little more sporty. I found him outside a flower shop on Nijo-dori. I'm not sure what he's doing peddeling flowers though.
Another view of the flower shop Sanders. One of my friends had this comment about this Sanders: "kentucky no ojisan, arubaito da~me da~me ne~". Which means she's not so big on the Colonel's part time job. My feeling is, "the colonel's got to do what the colonel's got to do."
This is a small Sanders. He's wearing yukata and has a fan that reads matsuri. He's ready to party. Matsuri! Not really a sign but he's included here to round out the Sanders Series.
This sign is posted along Kamo River. I like the "no golf" partly because I don't like golf that much and partly because I think it's funny that golfers would be enough of a problem in any public space that a no golfers sign would have to be posted. also, as the golfer pictured here is wearing special golf pants, i wonder your're not allowed to wear silly golf pants by the river. The X is in the pants area of the golfer...
This is an okonomiyaki shop in Teramachi, a pedestrian shopping area in the Kawaramachi-Shijo shopping district (related to one of my research projects). The name can't really hold a candle to captain santa but it's still pretty good.
This crab is also in Teramachi. It's animatronic! Maybe I'll take a series of pictures sometime and make an animation.
This sign is at the Daimon-ji trailhead. i like that the kid is yelling for help in English. The japanese says the area is dangerous (abunai!) and not to play there (koko de wa asobanai). i also like that the kids hat reads "danger." that kid could be nothing but trouble walking around in a hat like that. oh, also, the water on the other side is one or two inches deep.
